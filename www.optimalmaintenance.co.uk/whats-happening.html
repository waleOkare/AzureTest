Fundamental Maintenance Requirements in the UK

Did you know?

Most plants will be subject to regular inspections by the Health & Safety Executive within the UK. As far as maintenance is concerned they need to determine whether the maintenance function adequately targeted and adequately resourced in terms of people, time and finance. From a Major Accident Hazard perspective, this means;

  • Is the planning of SCE maintenance efficient?
  • Is the quality of maintenance acceptable?
  • Is the performance of SCEs reliable?
  • Are inspection and audit standards optimal?
  • Is supervisory and engineering support solid?

A maintenance management system should deliver the effective inspection, maintenance and testing activities that assess the condition of plant, detect deterioration and remedy the identified shortcomings. The legislative foundation is given below;

  • Offshore Installations (Offshore Safety Directive)(Safety Case etc.) Regulations 2015
  • Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations (PUWER) 1998
  • Offshore Installations Prevention of Fire and Explosion, and Emergency Response Regulations 1995 (PFEER)
  • Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations (MHSWR) 1999
  • Detailed legislative requirements are available on request.
